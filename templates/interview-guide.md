# Interview Guide Template

Use this to prepare for customer interviews focused on problem discovery.

---

## Context & Goals

**Problem We're Exploring:**
[Brief description of what you're trying to understand]

**Approach:**
We're trying to understand how you currently [solve/experience] [problem area]. 
We're not selling you anything; we want to learn from your experience.

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

---

## Screener Questions (5 min)

Use these to confirm you have the right participant:

1. Have you [experienced the problem area] in the last [timeframe]?
2. When did this last happen?
3. Do you have time for a [duration]-minute conversation about your experience?

---

## Background & Context (5 min)

Get to know them and their situation:

1. Tell me about your role and what you do.
2. How long have you been [in this role/using this product/dealing with this problem]?

---

## Core Discovery Questions (20-30 min)

**Behavioral:**

1. Walk me through the last time you [experienced the problem area]. Start from when you first realized you needed to [solve/address] this.
   - Probes: Where were you? What device/tools? How much time did it take? What was frustrating?

2. How do you currently [solve/approach] this situation?
   - Probes: What tools/steps? Who helps you? What works well? What's tedious?

3. What would need to change for you to [desired outcome]?
   - Probes: Is it speed? Simplicity? Trust? Something else?

**Emotional & Motivational:**

4. How does [problem area] affect you emotionally? Does it stress you, frustrate you, etc.?
   - Probes: How important is solving this to you? Would you change your behavior if [X] improved?

5. If you had to rank [problem area] against your other concerns, where would it fall?
   - Probes: What's more urgent/important? Why?

---

## Closing Questions (5 min)

1. Is there anything else about your [experience/workflow] I should know?

2. If we solved [specific aspect], would that meaningfully improve things for you?
   - Note: Ask about the problem, not the solution

3. Would you be willing to be contacted for follow-up questions?
   - Get contact info if yes

---

## Note-Taking Structure

During the interview, track:

- **Time spent:** [Total duration, segments]
- **Emotions observed:** [Frustration, stress, confidence, anxiety, etc.]
- **Behaviors:** [Hesitations, rereading, workarounds, asking for help]
- **Contradictions:** [What they said vs. what they showed]
- **Surprises:** [Unexpected information or emphasis]
- **Key quotes:** [With context, not cherry-picked]

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Leading questions: Avoid "Wouldn't it be great if..."
- Solution validation: "Would you use X?" (don't do this)
- Yes-biasing: Customer just agreeing to be polite
- PM talking too much: Let customer talk 80% of time
- Rushing: Let silences happen; people fill them with good information

---

## Post-Interview

After each interview:

1. Write 1-2 minute summary while memory is fresh
2. Note any patterns you're seeing across multiple interviews
3. Identify unanswered questions for next interview
4. Flag any contradictions to explore further
